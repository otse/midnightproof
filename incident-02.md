# 02
Settlers believe space is scary. There's no sunlight? You sleep in bunks and read computer screens. You're in space, not really living like anyone intended it. But you're alive. Alive?

I think my oxygen purification systems are offline. I'm a sigh short of dioxide, lost ideas bounce around. Floating up here is lonely, sure is, no settler would ever feel wrong about that. Because it always is. But lonely is not bad.

I am going to stop the figures of speeches and come to the point. I'm not lonely, I'm heading toward an anomaly, I used artificial intelligence to try and visualize it and to explain it, but it started hallucinating about that it's a singularity…I gave up fitting descriptors to the problem and instead used a pen and paper, and it seems the problem is that whatever I'm approaching is disintegrating.

That means the computer will identify it as a multiple. I redirect course to my intended destination using a language that looks like Arnold C, it sets extremes then suggests an alternative and is quite a strange paradigm.

After sleeping in my bunk, I feel confident. But maybe in a bad way. Things never ought to be positive. If you think it like that, you know you're lying. Space is a game of paradox. Whatever you want to happen will happen in an altered form of some consequence. Anyway, I have a coffee and a snack and feel better.

This is the mission. To fly to Barabas. I read a book about it, it appears as a place of abandon. I will have to pull in mineral and asteroid to sample and set more intelligent discourses that jockey toward the computers goal. Always the computer in the right, not my intuition or the punishment of my psychology. This has always been the way it is.

If I ever arrive, I can share what I learned. FTL. I will need to remain human to tell anyone what another place is like, you can't convince anyone if you’re post human, and my message isn't a transmission speaking of old tech and toasters.

My story is of perseverance. I am the explorer stuck in a prism of cosmic gamma. Whenever I think I see light, I actually just had a good meal and entertained myself. But the psychology isn't a layer of the mission it's sometimes feels like it's lost it.

If I ever find Barabas, or the disaster at Barabas, I will keep looking. Because I am certain that out there, there are those who follow. Those that remain. I can't be the only one who believes that modern tech, religion and board computers can plot course to a system of abandon. I turn the nob of the entertainment system and chuckle at my confidence in tech, the joke of my situation and the ever grimacing malice of the far and dark future.
