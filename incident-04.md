# Vidya and forums
It felt like I spawned on this displaced rock, my visor showing a spinning icon. Nothing but misinformation, loading data and guesses from the boarding personality. I toggle the device and have an honest look around, my intelligence taking over to discern what could be the ill-fated history of this place. There were actually two Suns, close in proximity.
I plowed for some time through a barren flat and saw a fata morgana. I couldn't make sense of it and discarded the idea that I found anything. My senses were of the only use here, with my sensors having taken a nosedive. I take my flacon and take a good sip. I later remembered I doses it with vitamins and compounds, and vaguely recognized the stimulants.
I walked for what seemed like hours when I happened upon a glass structure, or a glasshouse. I flip my augment and wipe my eyes, stinging with sweat and from exertion, having plotted on hill and depression to find someone living here. The idea that I wasn't going to die was comforting, my ship not having the fuel to find another place to land.
I help myself to enter the glasshouse and am met by bugs. Music is playing and there's metal buckets and hoses that are still running. I walk past rows of hydrophonic installations when a brick wall looms ahead like a fata morgana. It looks like a friendly house, not a rigged military forward operating base.
I knock on the door and are greeted by a humanoid butler who calls me sir. I tell it I’m lost, and briefly my mission. The unit seems only accustomed to one or two humans. I follow it as it promises me tea and cigarettes and we walk through a darkened hall onto a living room with lead in glass and luxury chairs. I plop in a chair and attempt to feel better.
A hospitable old man appears from the kitchen, gestures to the security system. I briefly look his way but he looks off to the side, not interested in eye contact or cues, already knowing the nature of my visit. I look off in front of me. The man plops down on a single sofa and leans back, and the robot comes around and brings us both green tea.
I try to relax and smell the aroma of the tea, peachy and grassy. I look in front of me, trying to imagine what harm this man could bring to me or what he could spike the drink with, but come up with blank silence and my senses relaxing. I drink the wonderful liquid and look at the picture frame of him and a woman. The man is lost in thought, looking up through the sky light.
I slide a technical slab his way with my fuel economy report and a stick with economical credits. He studies the data I presented and after some time, gestures to his robot and whispers something to its sensors, and visibly takes the security and puts it in his blouse. The robot comes back with two fuel cells, enough for the system.
