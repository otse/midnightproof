# 03
The crude circular apparatus is going twap, dots on the edges of the gauge. I look back at the expanse and discern what I believe are the objects of tech origin.
I flick the manual override and grab the stick, thrusting effortlessly to the impending objects. Twap. I swipe dust from the glass of the old implement that's been crudely affixed, revealing more dots. Twap. I glance at the heads up display but it's got nothing against old components like this, there's just something about field near communication. Twap.

The objects reflect nearby stars as they spin, like diamonds almost. I use a mining implement to prospect the objects. Mostly metal I think. I hover closer to the field and match the speed while keeping the collision computers running. I'm at eye distance now, and discern the objects more clearly. I stop the object from spinning and see it better. This is a part of a big class likely to come off during collision. I clear away from the objects, using compressed air. I notice the glistering of leaked oil, which takes weeks to oxidize. This is a current event then.

I tap the board mascot button and it starts talking about the event that just occurred. It says it's not uncommon for our position in the system to see parts like this. Apparently I am in range of debris flung from a belt. That makes sense. The map shows the slingshot effect from a nearby planet as the cause for the incident.
I crack up at the southern voice of the mascot, but the mood quickly depresses again. I make sure the awareness systems are up then move to the coffee machine, which I try to ration using a pen and paper. I don't have a lot of it left, but it's an effective mood enhancer. Better than vitamins.

The mission isn't that easy anymore with others in the area. I have to worry about encounters. Maybe a corporation with a destruction quota. Robotic beings. I lean back in the padded commandant chair and gaze outside, coffee steaming into the cabin, life support leds flashing.
